Our Story Two Men and a Cat

12,754 kilometres, 7,925 miles, 16 airports, 4 countries, 2 continents, and far too many conferences. Here’s a little about the both of us and how we ended up together.

About Josh

Josh is a short and silly human born the youngest of four sons and raised in the North Bay. He’s a pathological over-committer and armchair philosopher who is, allegedly, a fan of the great outdoors. Josh spent too much of his life in the closet and f(l)ailing as a freelance web developer before giving in to the queer agenda and finding his niche as a community organizer. About the Cat

It is unclear whether Josh has a cat, or whether a cat has Josh. Nonetheless, that cat’s name is Pico and he is a force to be reckoned with.

How We Met

We met in July of 2014 in Portland, Oregon at OSCON, a conference run by Josh’s employer that Chris was speaking at. One night before the event Josh stumbled into a bunch of Taswegians at the hotel bar – he recognized them to be speakers and authors. They spent the evening regaling Josh with stories of Aussie politics and detailing the many ways a person could die in Australia (strangely there was never any mention of drop bears).

Chris thought Josh was awfully cute and made several passes at Josh throughout the week, but Josh was thoroughly overwhelmed by his work and completely oblivious to Chris’s overtures. Happily, Josh thought Chris was pretty damn cute too and they spent the next 12 months chatting each other up online.

July of 2015 rolled around and, with it, another OSCON. Chris wasn’t going to let Josh’s obliviousness get in the way this time – he devised a clever plan to have dinner with a small group of friends. Cleverness be damned, his plan was quickly foiled by an onslaught of wonderful people. Mercifully, Chris only had to wait another day before he got some time alone with Josh... and they’ve been together ever since.

After That...

A long distance relationship between Australia and America means taking flights across the Pacific Ocean. Lots of flights across the Pacific Ocean, in fact. In May of 2016, we decided we’d had enough of that. We got engaged and set our course for Chris to move in with Josh (and Pico) in Petaluma.

After ten months and lots of immigration paperwork, Chris made the move. We’ve been living together ever since, and we’re really looking forward to you joining in a very special part of our story.
